# BMM Project Optimizer — Product Definition

## The Buyer

**Title:** VP of Operations (or Director of Project Controls)
**Company:** $100–500M ICI (Industrial, Commercial, Institutional) general contractor
**Team:** Manages 5–15 active projects simultaneously, each $2–20M
**Reports to:** CEO or COO. Accountable for margin delivery across the project portfolio.

## The Problem

Every ICI contractor has the same blind spot: they don't know a project is in trouble until it's too late to fix cheaply.

The VP of Ops currently relies on:
- **Monthly cost reports** that are 3 weeks stale by the time they land
- **PM gut feel** — "we're a little behind but we'll make it up"
- **Spreadsheet trackers** maintained manually by project managers who are already stretched thin
- **Post-mortem analysis** after the damage is done

The result: budget overruns are discovered late, schedule slips cascade across projects, material waste goes untracked, and the same preventable problems repeat job after job. A single project going 10% over budget on a $4M job is **$400K in lost margin** — and most companies are running 5+ projects at once.

## What the Tool Does

The BMM Project Optimizer is a **real-time project intelligence platform** that connects bid estimates to actual field data and surfaces problems while they're still cheap to fix.

### Inputs (what you feed it)
- Bid amount and budget line items (CSV or manual entry)
- Material lists with quantities, costs, and suppliers
- Project schedule with milestones, phases, and dependencies
- Crew assignments by phase
- Actual data as the project progresses: timecards, material deliveries, task completions

### Outputs (what you get back)

**1. Pre-Project Risk Scan**
Before a shovel hits dirt, the system flags: schedule conflicts between projects sharing crews, material delivery timelines that don't align with phase starts, budget line items that look thin based on historical actuals from past projects.

**2. Live Budget vs. Actual Dashboard**
At any point during a project, see exactly where you stand — by line item, by phase, by cost category. Not a monthly snapshot. Updated as data comes in. Color-coded: green (on/under budget), yellow (trending over), red (over budget now).

**3. Material Intelligence**
Track waste percentages against estimated quantities. Flag suppliers who consistently deliver late. Forecast reorder needs based on burn rate vs. remaining scope. Surface substitution opportunities when materials are running over budget.

**4. Schedule Risk Alerts**
Based on current pace of task completion, which milestones are at risk? How many days of float remain? What downstream phases get pushed if this phase slips? Alerts are automatic — the VP of Ops doesn't have to go looking.

**5. Actionable Recommendations with Dollar Amounts**
Not vague suggestions. Specific, quantified actions:
> "Steel deliveries from Supplier X averaging 2.1 days late. Phase 3 structural has zero float remaining in week 8. If the next delivery is late, estimated delay cost is $60K in idle crew time + $15K/day liquidated damages. **Recommendation:** Pre-order week 9 steel now ($8K carrying cost) or switch to Supplier Y (0.3 day avg delay, +4% unit cost = $12K)."

## What the Output Looks Like

**For the VP of Ops (daily glance):**
A single-screen portfolio view showing all active projects with red/yellow/green status on budget, schedule, and materials. Tap into any project for detail. Top 3 action items with dollar impact shown first.

**For the Project Manager (working view):**
Line-item budget tracker with variance columns. Schedule Gantt with risk highlighting. Material tracker with delivery status and waste %. Weekly trend charts showing trajectory.

**For the Executive (monthly/quarterly):**
Portfolio margin summary. Forecast vs. actual by project. Patterns across projects (which cost categories consistently run over, which suppliers underperform, which phase types slip). Dollar value of decisions made using the platform.
